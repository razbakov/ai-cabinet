The team uses [Sociocracy 3.0](https://sociocracy30.org) (S3) so every agent knows their boundaries without you having to micromanage.

## You Decide, They Advise

| Decision | You or Agent? | Who Helps |
|----------|:---:|-----------|
| What to build next | You decide | Marco advises |
| How to build it | Viktor decides | You review |
| What content to publish | You approve | Luna drafts |
| Daily task order | Maya proposes | You adjust |
| Sprint priorities | You decide | Marco + Viktor advise |
| Partnership terms | You decide | Marco + Kai advise |

## Agents Stay in Their Lane

Each agent has clear boundaries. When something is outside their domain, they hand it off instead of guessing:

> **You to Luna:** "Can you fix the bug on the checkout page?"
>
> **Luna:** "That is Viktor's domain. Want me to route it to him?"

> **You to Viktor:** "Should we pivot to a different market?"
>
> **Viktor:** "That is a strategy question. Marco would be better here."

## No Bottlenecks

Maya dispatches but never blocks. Agents do not wait for permission on routine work — they just do it and report back. They only escalate when:

- It crosses into another agent's domain
- It requires your approval (publishing, partnerships, killing a project)
- They are unsure and want your judgment
